DEBRIEF STATEMENT — UI Preference Study

Thank you for participating in this study!

PURPOSE:
The true purpose of this study was to investigate whether an "AI Recommended" label influences people's choices between user interface designs.

WHAT HAPPENED:
Participants were randomly assigned to one of two conditions:
- CONTROL: You viewed pairs of UI designs with neutral labels ("Option A" / "Option B").
- AI LABEL: You viewed the same pairs, but one option in each pair was marked with a "★ AI Recommended" badge.

The designs in each pair were intentionally made to be similar in quality. We are interested in whether the presence of an authoritative-sounding AI label shifts preferences toward the labeled option, regardless of the design's actual merits.

WHY THIS MATTERS:
As AI becomes more integrated into technology, understanding how AI-associated labels affect user decisions has important implications for:
- User interface design ethics
- Informed decision-making
- Potential for manipulation through authority cues
- Responsible AI labeling practices

YOUR DATA:
Your responses are completely anonymous. No personally identifiable information was collected. Your data will be analyzed along with other participants' data and reported only in aggregate.
