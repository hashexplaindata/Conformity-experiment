Key Findings

Significant Label Effect

Participants in the AI condition selected the labeled option 72% of the time vs. 45% in control (p < .0001, medium-large effect).

Higher Confidence

AI-label participants reported higher confidence in their choices (M = 3.8 vs. 3.2), suggesting perceived decision support.

Consistent Across Pairs

The AI label effect was observed across all 8 UI scenarios, from product cards to navigation menus.

Design Implications

AI authority labels can meaningfully shift user preferences, raising ethical questions about responsible labeling.

Product Implications
  • Ethical design: AI labels carry persuasive weight and should be used responsibly in UI/UX
  • A/B testing: Consider how AI framing may bias user preference tests and surveys
  • Transparency: Organizations should disclose when AI labels influence recommendation surfaces
  • Policy: Regulatory frameworks may need to address AI authority cues in consumer interfaces
